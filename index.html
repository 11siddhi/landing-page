Some Jelly-tastic Facts You Didn’t Know!

Jellyfish have been around for over 500 million years, making them one of the oldest living creatures on Earth—predating dinosaurs, trees, and even sharks!

Jellyfish lack a brain, heart, and even bones. Instead, they have a simple nerve net that helps them respond to changes in their environment.

Many jellyfish can glow in the dark through a process called bioluminescence. This ability helps them communicate, lure prey, or ward off predators.

Not all jellyfish stings are deadly, but some, like the Box jellyfish, can be extremely dangerous. Their venom can cause intense pain, paralysis, and even cardiac arrest in humans.
